Attractions

One of the main things that attracts many tourists to London are the main attractions. These include the London eye, Big Ben and London Bridge. There are also many more like the stadiums all over London, including the Olympic stadium built specifically for the London 2012 Olympics. Let's not forget the royalty living here in London; the queen is not too far away with Buckingham Palace here in central London. Though it may cost a few pounds (UK's currency) to visit some of these attractions, it would definetly be worth it. Or, another great idea, look at it from the outside and take a few pictures.

Parks

We also have the 8 royal parks, such as Hyde Park, Greenwich park and many others spread around London. These are usually free to access, so every once in a while when we do get that sunny day, why not enjoy the greenery by having yourself a lovely picnic. There also other gardens such as kew gardens, which also charges but is definetly worth seeing.

Galleries and Museums

Let's not forget history, you also have the oppurtunity to visit some of the greatest galleries and museums in London, such as the British Museum, Horniman Museum, Tate Gallery and the National Art Gallery. Most of these are free to access, especially as an art lover or historian, one can really enjoy the lovely selection available

Theatres

We also house some very spectacular productions, so why not visit the London theatres and watch shows like Lion King, Wicked and Matilda (amongst many). Though at a cost, it would defiently be worth the wait and money.

Seasonal Attractions

Instead of just enjoying the great attractions of London, why not see them at their peaks? During winter London lights up, we are able to experience a christmas themed London, with a glowing London eye and Tower bridge but also shop with beautiful ornaments in the air in Oxford Street. Not only in the winter but in the summer some of the the royal parks are able to host some world class events and concerts like Wireless!!
